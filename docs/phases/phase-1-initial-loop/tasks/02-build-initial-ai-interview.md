# Build the initial AI interview

Status: in-progress

Owner: primary-agent

Depends on: Define the life-map domain

## Outcome

Create a resumable first-use conversation with a neutral, calm-friend voice that explains Mindland in one sentence, immediately begins with a broad invitation, learns what matters to the user, and produces structured proposals for the meaningful specific islands it discovers. Three to five is typical, while fewer are allowed when the conversation supports fewer. Candidate islands form quietly behind the mist-covered chat as they are discovered, while their names remain hidden.

## Done when

The interview opens broadly, asks one question per message, streams replies, adapts for roughly five to twelve messages, feels conversational, preserves the user’s words, produces validated structured output, avoids inventing filler islands, handles unclear answers safely, and creates no scored activity. Every user and assistant message is stored privately in Convex and an interrupted session resumes. It shows a percentage-style progress bar, summarizes its understanding for corrections through chat, and ends with a Create my map button. Tapping it dissolves the native glass controls and shared mist layer to reveal the islands and their names, with accessible reduced-transparency and reduced-motion fallbacks.
